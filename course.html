Abacus Class

ABACUS CLASS

The abacus consists of a frame with beads or discs that can be moved up and down on wire rods. Each row of beads represents a different value; the bottom row typically represents units, the next row tens, and so on. To perform a calculation, the user manipulates the beads to create the desired number and then uses their fingers to count the total.

Abacus is an excellent tool for improving the brain’s processing speed – working memory and long-term memory. This can help students learn faster than if they were reading or listening. Abacus enhances the brain’s processing speed to quickly and efficiently process information. The visual aspect of the abacus also helps with this because it requires more concentration on what’s being done vs. writing out information on paper or typing it into a computer.

ATL Class

ATL CLASS

The Atal Tinkering Lab (ATL) initiative in India aims to foster curiosity, creativity, and imagination in young minds, and inculcate skills such as design mind-set, computational thinking, adaptive learning, physical computing, and so on. The benefits to students include exposure to cutting-edge technologies, hands-on experience in solving real-world problems, and the opportunity to develop innovative solutions. ATLs also provide a platform for students to collaborate, experiment, and learn through practical application, thereby nurturing a spirit of entrepreneurship and innovation.
